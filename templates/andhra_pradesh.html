Andhra Pradesh - Land of Temples and Forts

Explore the rich history, temples, and majestic forts of Andhra Pradesh.

Hampi

Hampi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Karnataka, India, is a captivating ancient city renowned for its stunning ruins and rich historical significance. Once the flourishing capital of the Vijayanagara Empire in the 14th century, Hampi features an impressive collection of temples, palaces, and market streets set amidst dramatic boulder-strewn landscapes. Key attractions include the Virupaksha Temple, the Vittala Temple complex with its iconic Stone Chariot, and the Lotus Mahal. The site’s architectural splendor and historical depth offer a fascinating glimpse into the grandeur of one of South India's greatest empires.

Amaravati Stupa Sculpture

The Amaravati Stupa Sculptures, originating from the ancient city of Amaravati in Andhra Pradesh, India, are renowned for their exquisite depiction of Buddhist art and iconography. Dating back to the 2nd century BCE to the 3rd century CE, these sculptures are part of the large stupa complex that served as a significant center of Buddhist worship. The carvings on the stupa’s railings, gateways, and pillars illustrate various scenes from the life of Buddha, Jataka tales (stories of Buddha’s previous lives), and intricate floral and geometric patterns. The Amaravati sculptures are notable for their detailed craftsmanship and serve as a vital historical record of early Buddhist art and the spread of Buddhism in India.
